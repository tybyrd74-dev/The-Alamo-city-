About San Antonio

San Antonio is a vibrant city where history, culture, and modern life meet. Beyond the Alamo, the city is known for the River Walk, rich Tejano and Mexican heritage, and lively festivals year-round. The city blends historic neighborhoods with growing culinary and arts scenes.

Visitor information

  • Hours: The Alamo church and grounds are generally open daily; hours vary seasonally ‚Äî check the official site before visiting.
  • Admission: General admission to the Alamo church is often free, while special exhibits, guided tours, or the Alamo Gardens may require tickets or donations.
  • Guided tours: Ranger-led and private guided tours are available and provide deeper historical context.
  • Best time to visit: Weekday mornings are quieter. Spring and fall offer mild weather; summers can be very hot.

Getting there & nearby

  • Transit: VIA Metropolitan Transit serves downtown San Antonio. The River Walk and downtown are walkable from the Alamo.
  • Parking: Several paid parking lots and garages surround Alamo Plaza; expect higher demand during events.
  • Nearby attractions: San Antonio River Walk, San Fernando Cathedral, La Villita Historic Arts Village, and the San Antonio Museum of Art are all within a short walk.

For official visitor details and up-to-date hours, see the Alamo official website and the City of San Antonio.
